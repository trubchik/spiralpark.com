The Island · The Moment

Madeira Is Ready. So Are We.

Year-round warmth, direct flights from 30+ European cities, Portugal's welcoming tax framework — Madeira is fast becoming the destination for experience-seekers looking beyond Bali and Ibiza. But quality experiential venues here are almost nonexistent. Spiral Park sits on 2.4 ha of owned forest hillside, 15 minutes from Funchal city centre, 18 minutes from the airport — already drawing guests who fly in just for our events.

Madeira is rapidly emerging as a destination for experiential tourism, remote work communities and wellness travel. This creates growing demand for unique venues and cultural destinations beyond traditional hotels.

  • Live revenue, zero paid marketing, 65% repeat rate
  • Growing international audience — guests fly to Madeira for Spiral
  • Tourism construction rights confirmed on-site

Risk Mitigation

Risks & How We Address Them

Portuguese bureaucracy
Risk: Delays in legalization. Mitigation: Planned infrastructure changes to generate operating revenue do not require permits.
Founder dependency
Risk: Currently founder-led. Mitigation: Spiral Park is already an established platform: a hard asset + a local community of practitioners. Events already run with external facilitators. The founder remains the creative catalyst, but not the sole carrier of project value.
Demand concentration
Risk: Guest base concentrated in word-of-mouth. Mitigation: Post-launch: local, international, content-driven, and corporate channels. No single-channel dependency.
Construction overrun
Risk: Costs exceed budget. Mitigation: Phased build (The Egg first). Fixed-price contracts. 15% contingency. Accommodation is optional — not required for break-even.
Seasonality
Risk: Weather dependence. Mitigation: Madeira's year-round climate. The Egg is indoor. Corporate bookings fill off-peak months.
Competitive entry
Risk: New competitors. Mitigation: No direct competitor within 30 min of Funchal. Land scarcity is a barrier. Formats, brand, and community compound over time.
Exit Strategies
Founder Buyback (Year 3–5) Pre-agreed formula: 2× at year 3, declining to 1.5× at year 5. Investor right, not obligation.
Hospitality Operator Sale Sale to a wellness group, boutique operator, or Madeira-focused developer. Land sets the floor; track record adds a revenue multiple.
Structured Refinance Refinance against asset value in Year 2–3. Partial liquidity for the investor while the company keeps operating.
Long-Term Cashflow Hold Hold equity, receive ongoing distributions. No mandatory exit. Suited for cashflow-oriented investors.
